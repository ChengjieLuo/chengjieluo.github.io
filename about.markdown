I earned my Ph.D. in the Non-Equilibrium Soft Matter group, led by [Liesbeth Janssen](https://tps.phys.tue.nl/janssen/), at the Department of Applied Physics, Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e). Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Theory of Biological Fluids group, led by [David Zwicker](https://www.zwickergroup.org/david-zwicker), at the Max Planck Institute for Dynamics and Self-Organization (MPI-DS).

My research focuses on non-equilibrium statistical physics, soft matter, and biological systems. Using a combination of theoretical approaches and simulations, I aim to uncover the principles governing liquid–liquid phase separation, liquid–glass transitions, and the dynamics of complex fluids. I am particularly intrigued by the coexistence and interfaces between distinct phases, exploring how subtle interactions and asymmetries give rise to rich, emergent behaviors.

In addition to traditional physics, I am interested in drawing conceptual parallels between physical interfaces and human–AI interfaces. My goal is to understand how "interfaces" and "boundaries" influence the structure, dynamics, and evolution of a future society where humans and AI coexist.
