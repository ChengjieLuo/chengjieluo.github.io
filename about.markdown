I obtained my Ph.D. from the Non-Equilibrium Soft Matter group led by Liesbeth Janssen at the Department of Applied Physics, Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e). I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in the Theory of Biological Fluids group led by David Zwicker at the Max Planck Institute for Dynamics and Self-Organization (MPI-DS).

My research explores non-equilibrium statistical physics, soft matter, and biological systems, using a combination of theory and simulation to uncover the principles behind liquid–liquid phase separation, liquid–glass transition, and the dynamics of complex fluids. I am particularly fascinated by the coexistence and interfaces between distinct phases, investigating how subtle interactions and asymmetry give rise to rich, emergent behavior.

Beyond traditional physics, I plan to explore conceptual parallels between physical interfaces and human–AI interactions, aiming to understand how "interfaces" and "boundaries" shape the structure, dynamics, and evolution of a future human–AI coexistent society.
